1.2 Methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

Following on from comments in 1.1, the primary skill needed to complete any digital project is a real understanding of the business to which it applies.

Gall's law [1] states:-

"A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. A complex system designed from scratch never works and cannot be patched up to make it work. You have to start over with a working simple system."

Ignoring how people work with the existing system and trying to implement or impose a completely new way of working without extensive consultation and/or training is a recipe for disaster.

Secondary skills are an understanding of the packages that will be used (e.g. html script if designing a website)

The resources used need to be matched to the application and pre-existing infrastructure: you do not need state of the art graphics cards in computers used for an accounting application

[1] General systemantics : an essay on how systems work, and especially how they fail, 1975. , John Gall (September 18, 1925 - December 15, 2014) - American author and pediatrician
